# Embodied Simulation: A Synthesis

The convergence of embodied cognition and mental representation through simulation offers a unified framework for understanding how minds model reality. This synthesis bridges the apparent gap between action-oriented and representation-oriented theories of cognition.

## The Simulation Hypothesis

Simulation serves as the meeting point between embodied and representational views:

**Embodied Perspective**: Cognition is grounded in sensorimotor experience. Understanding concepts requires activating the same neural systems used in perception and action.

**Representational Perspective**: Cognition operates on internal models that represent world states. These representations support reasoning, planning, and prediction.

**Synthesis**: Mental simulation uses embodied systems to run internal representations. Imagining an action activates motor systems. Understanding a scene activates perceptual systems. The representations are embodied.

## Simulation as Cognitive Mechanism

Mental simulation operates across cognitive domains:

### Motor Simulation
Planning movements involves simulating them internally. Motor imagery activates premotor cortex. Athletes use mental practice because simulation and execution share neural substrates.

### Perceptual Simulation
Visual imagery engages visual cortex. Imagining sounds activates auditory regions. Perceptual representation is perceptual simulation.

### Social Simulation
Understanding others involves simulating their mental states. Mirror neurons suggest we comprehend actions by internally simulating them. Theory of mind may be embodied simulation of other minds.

### Temporal Simulation
Imagining future scenarios involves constructive simulation. Episodic future thinking and episodic memory share neural mechanisms. We simulate futures using the same systems that reconstruct pasts.

## Multiple Representation Formats

Simulation supports multiple representational formats:

**Explicit States**: Discrete, articulable representations of specific situations. Conscious access to simulated content.

**Implicit Dynamics**: Procedural knowledge embedded in simulation processes. Know-how that emerges through simulated action.

**Probabilistic Distributions**: Uncertainty represented through variation in simulated outcomes. Multiple simulation runs explore possibility space.

**Hierarchical Structures**: Simulations at multiple levels of abstraction. High-level plans decompose into detailed simulated actions.

## World Models as Simulation Engines

The world model framework naturally incorporates embodied simulation:

World models are learned simulators. They capture dynamics that generate predictions. Running the world model forward simulates possible futures.

Embodied world models ground simulation in sensorimotor experience. The simulation process uses the same representational formats as perception and action.

This explains why imagination feels experiential - it literally runs on experiential systems.

## Implications for AI

Artificial systems can implement embodied simulation principles:

**Learned Simulators**: Train models to predict sensorimotor consequences of actions. Use these as internal simulation engines.

**Embodied Representations**: Ground abstract concepts in simulated sensorimotor experience. Connect symbols to simulation.

**Active Inference**: Use simulation to plan actions that reduce prediction error. Act to make the world match internal models.

**Mental Practice**: Train systems through simulated experience, not just real interaction. Exploit simulation for sample-efficient learning.

## Unifying Framework

Embodied simulation synthesis resolves apparent contradictions:

- Cognition is both embodied AND representational - representations are embodied simulations
- Cognition is both situated AND abstract - abstraction emerges from situated simulation
- Cognition is both online AND offline - offline cognition simulates online processing

The key insight: representation and embodiment are not opposites. Mental representations are structured patterns of simulated embodied experience.
