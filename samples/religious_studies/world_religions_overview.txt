# World Religions: Comparative Overview

## Introduction

Religious traditions shape cultures, ethics, and worldviews for billions of people worldwide. This overview examines major world religions, their core beliefs, practices, and historical development, approaching each tradition with scholarly objectivity and respect.

## Abrahamic Religions

### Judaism

**Origins:** Ancient Near East, traditionally traced to Abraham (~2000 BCE)

**Sacred Texts:**
- Torah (Pentateuch): Five Books of Moses
- Nevi'im: Prophets
- Ketuvim: Writings
- Together form the Tanakh (Hebrew Bible)
- Talmud: Rabbinic commentary and law

**Core Beliefs:**
- Monotheism: One God (YHWH)
- Covenant: Special relationship between God and Jewish people
- Torah: Divine instruction for righteous living
- Messianic hope: Future redemption

**Practices:**
- Shabbat: Weekly day of rest
- Kosher: Dietary laws
- Prayer: Three daily services
- Life cycle rituals: Brit milah, Bar/Bat Mitzvah
- Holidays: Passover, Yom Kippur, Rosh Hashanah

**Major Branches:**
- Orthodox: Traditional observance
- Conservative: Modern with tradition
- Reform: Liberal adaptation
- Reconstructionist: Judaism as evolving civilization

### Christianity

**Origins:** 1st century CE Palestine, based on teachings of Jesus of Nazareth

**Sacred Texts:**
- Old Testament (Hebrew Bible)
- New Testament: Gospels, Acts, Epistles, Revelation

**Core Beliefs:**
- Trinity: One God in three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit)
- Incarnation: Jesus as God made human
- Salvation: Redemption through Christ's death and resurrection
- Grace: Unmerited divine favor
- Second Coming: Return of Christ

**Practices:**
- Sacraments: Baptism, Eucharist (and others depending on tradition)
- Sunday worship
- Prayer and scripture reading
- Holy days: Christmas, Easter, Pentecost

**Major Branches:**
- Catholic: Papal authority, 1.3 billion adherents
- Orthodox: Eastern traditions, ecumenical patriarchs
- Protestant: Reformation traditions (Lutheran, Reformed, Baptist, Methodist, etc.)
- Pentecostal: Emphasis on Holy Spirit gifts

### Islam

**Origins:** 7th century CE Arabia, Prophet Muhammad

**Sacred Texts:**
- Qur'an: Direct revelation to Muhammad
- Hadith: Sayings and actions of the Prophet
- Sunnah: Prophetic tradition

**Core Beliefs (Six Articles of Faith):**
- Tawhid: Oneness of God (Allah)
- Angels: Spiritual beings
- Books: Revealed scriptures
- Prophets: Messengers from Adam to Muhammad
- Day of Judgment: Accountability and afterlife
- Qadar: Divine decree

**Five Pillars:**
1. Shahada: Declaration of faith
2. Salat: Five daily prayers
3. Zakat: Almsgiving
4. Sawm: Fasting during Ramadan
5. Hajj: Pilgrimage to Mecca

**Major Branches:**
- Sunni: Majority tradition (~85%)
- Shi'a: Follow Ali and Imams (~15%)
- Sufi: Mystical tradition (within both)

## Dharmic Religions

### Hinduism

**Origins:** Indus Valley civilization and Vedic traditions (~1500 BCE)

**Sacred Texts:**
- Vedas: Oldest scriptures (Rig, Sama, Yajur, Atharva)
- Upanishads: Philosophical texts
- Bhagavad Gita: Song of the Lord
- Epics: Mahabharata, Ramayana
- Puranas: Mythological stories

**Core Concepts:**
- Brahman: Ultimate reality
- Atman: Individual soul
- Dharma: Cosmic order and duty
- Karma: Law of action and consequence
- Samsara: Cycle of rebirth
- Moksha: Liberation from cycle

**Practices:**
- Puja: Worship and offerings
- Yoga: Paths to liberation
- Pilgrimage: Sacred sites
- Festivals: Diwali, Holi, Navaratri

**Major Traditions:**
- Vaishnavism: Vishnu-centered
- Shaivism: Shiva-centered
- Shaktism: Goddess-centered
- Smartism: Multiple deities

### Buddhism

**Origins:** 6th-5th century BCE India, Siddhartha Gautama (Buddha)

**Sacred Texts:**
- Tripitaka (Pali Canon): Vinaya, Sutta, Abhidhamma
- Mahayana sutras: Heart Sutra, Lotus Sutra
- Tibetan Kangyur and Tengyur

**Core Teachings:**
- Four Noble Truths:
  1. Dukkha: Suffering exists
  2. Samudaya: Suffering has a cause (craving)
  3. Nirodha: Suffering can end
  4. Magga: Path to end suffering

- Noble Eightfold Path:
  Right view, intention, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, concentration

- Three Marks of Existence:
  Impermanence (anicca), suffering (dukkha), non-self (anatta)

**Practices:**
- Meditation: Samatha (calm), vipassana (insight)
- Ethical conduct: Five precepts
- Monastic life: Sangha community
- Merit-making: Generosity, offerings

**Major Branches:**
- Theravada: "Way of the Elders" (Southeast Asia)
- Mahayana: "Great Vehicle" (East Asia)
- Vajrayana: Tantric Buddhism (Tibet, Mongolia)

### Sikhism

**Origins:** 15th century CE Punjab, Guru Nanak

**Sacred Text:**
- Guru Granth Sahib: Living Guru, contains writings of Sikh Gurus and saints

**Core Beliefs:**
- Ik Onkar: One God
- Equality: All humans equal regardless of caste, gender
- Service: Seva to community
- Honest living: Kirat karo
- Sharing: Vand chakko

**Five K's:**
- Kesh: Uncut hair
- Kangha: Wooden comb
- Kara: Steel bracelet
- Kachera: Cotton undergarment
- Kirpan: Ceremonial dagger

**Practices:**
- Daily prayers: Nitnem
- Langar: Free community kitchen
- Gurdwara: Place of worship
- Naam Simran: Meditation on divine name

## East Asian Religions

### Confucianism

**Origins:** 6th-5th century BCE China, Confucius (Kong Qiu)

**Core Texts:** Analects, Five Classics

**Key Concepts:**
- Ren: Benevolence, humaneness
- Li: Ritual propriety, etiquette
- Xiao: Filial piety
- Junzi: Exemplary person
- Zhengming: Rectification of names

**Five Relationships:**
Ruler-subject, parent-child, husband-wife, elder-younger sibling, friend-friend

### Daoism (Taoism)

**Origins:** 6th-4th century BCE China

**Core Texts:**
- Dao De Jing (Laozi)
- Zhuangzi

**Key Concepts:**
- Dao (Tao): The Way, ultimate principle
- Wu wei: Non-action, effortless action
- Yin-Yang: Complementary forces
- Ziran: Naturalness
- De: Virtue, power

**Practices:**
- Meditation and breathing exercises
- Tai chi and qigong
- Temple rituals
- Internal alchemy

### Shinto

**Origins:** Indigenous religion of Japan

**Core Concepts:**
- Kami: Sacred spirits in nature, ancestors, concepts
- Purity and pollution
- Harmony with nature
- Ancestral reverence

**Practices:**
- Shrine worship
- Purification rituals
- Festivals (matsuri)
- Offerings

## Indigenous and Traditional Religions

Diverse traditions worldwide sharing common features:
- Connection to land and nature
- Ancestral veneration
- Oral traditions
- Ritual specialists (shamans, elders)
- Community-centered practice

## Comparative Themes

| Theme | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Ultimate Reality | God, Brahman, Dao, Buddha-nature |
| Human Problem | Sin, Ignorance, Suffering, Disharmony |
| Solution | Salvation, Enlightenment, Liberation |
| Practice | Prayer, Meditation, Ritual, Ethics |
| Community | Church, Sangha, Ummah, Gurdwara |

## Scholarly Approaches

- **Phenomenology:** Describe religious experience
- **Comparative:** Identify patterns across traditions
- **Historical:** Trace development over time
- **Sociological:** Examine social functions
- **Anthropological:** Study cultural context
